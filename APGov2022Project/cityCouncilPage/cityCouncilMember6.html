Jack Walser, Councilmember At-Large

John "Jack" Walser was appointed to the City Council on July 13, 2021, filling a vacancy left by Tim Goodrich who resigned prior to his full term. Jack currently serves as the Chair of the Citizen Development and Enrichment Committee, and also as a member of the Community Planning and Design Committee and Transportation Committee. He also serves as an alternate for State Legislative Advocacy. Prior to his Council appointment, Jack was employed by Mobil Oil at the Torrance Refinery for 10 years as an Industrial Engineer, Maintenance Supervisor, Maintenance Planning Superintendent and Long Range Economics & Planning Supervisor. He also served on the Torrance Water Commission, Salvation Army Torrance Corps Advisory Board, Torrance Area Chamber of Commerce, and as a Volunteer Ski Instructor for the disabled through the United States Adaptive Recreation Center. Jack is a graduate of California State Polytechnic University with a B.S.I.E. Industrial Engineering and an MBA from UCLA Graduate School of Management Marketing and Finance. Jack has owned and operated WALSER’S, a family-run art materials, office supplies and now tactical firearms and ammunition store, since 1975. For over a dozen years, WALSER’S was the designated electronic recycling agent for the City of Torrance and has been widely recognized for their waste reduction efforts through the years. Jack has been a Torrance resident for more than 50 years. He and his wife, Janey, raised two wonderful children while she taught in the Torrance Unified School District.
